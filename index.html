§ 01 · The Present Tense

Currently building.

Updated · May 2026

My current obsession is harness engineering on Claude Code — the soft machinery that lets a language model actually do things on your behalf rather than just talk about them. Tool definitions, agent loops, LLM-as-judge eval rigs, the alignment tape that keeps an autonomous run from veering off the road. That whole stack.

The reason I care: I'm trying to figure out how much of my own work I can do AFK — away from keyboard. Not in the lazy sense. In the tell-the-machine-where-you're-going-and-come-back-to-a-pull-request sense. The past few years of my career have been about helping teams ship ML reliably; this next chapter feels like the inverse — building the harness that lets a small number of humans direct a large amount of agentic work, without the work ever feeling out of control.

So I read a lot of agent SDKs. I write a lot of evals. I think a lot about the seams between what a model is good at and what it isn't, and where a well-placed judge or guardrail turns a flaky pipeline into a useful one. If any of that sounds like the shape of your own week — I'd love to compare notes.

Glossary

Harness engineering — the scaffolding around an LLM: tools, evals, judges, alignment, the loop itself. Less glamorous than model training. More important for shipping.

AFK

Away from keyboard. The promise that you can describe a problem, walk away, and find a credible solution waiting when you come back. Mostly aspirational, sometimes real.

Reading

Lately: papers on tool use, agent benchmarks, and anything Anthropic ships about claude-code.
